# CSRD — Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive

EU 2022/2464. Replaced Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD). Mandatory sustainability reporting for large EU + non-EU companies operating in EU.

## Scope (phased)

- 2024 (FY24 reporting): large EU companies already subject to NFRD (~12K companies)
- 2025: large EU companies not previously subject (~50K companies)
- 2026: listed SMEs
- 2028: non-EU parent companies with significant EU presence

## What must be reported

- Sustainability information per ESRS (European Sustainability Reporting Standards)
- Double materiality: financial + impact
- Mandatory third-party assurance (limited at first, reasonable later)

## Cash mgmt link

- Treasury contributes data to corporate CSRD report (financing, deposits, hedging activities)
- Banks themselves report under CSRD
- ESG-linked products' KPI verification overlaps with CSRD audit

## ESRS

- Set of 12 standards (Cross-cutting, E1-5, S1-4, G1)
- E1 = climate change
- E5 = circular economy
- S1 = own workforce
- G1 = business conduct
