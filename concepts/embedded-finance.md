# Embedded finance

Non-financial brand offering financial services via partnerships with regulated providers. Bank capabilities embedded in third-party products.

## Examples

- Uber paying drivers via embedded debit + instant payout
- Shopify Capital lending to merchants
- Apple Card / Pay (via Goldman / banks)
- Amazon paying suppliers via embedded SCF
- Treasury-as-a-service in B2B SaaS (e.g., Mercury, Modern Treasury)

## Why corps want it

- Customer experience uplift (one-click checkout, instant payout)
- Revenue from financial services (interchange, FX spread)
- Customer retention + data
- Competitive differentiation

## Why banks engage

- New distribution channel
- Volume from non-bank brands
- Product innovation pressure
- Defense against fintech disintermediation

## Common patterns

- **BaaS** ([[baas]]) — bank rents charter to fintech / brand
- **API-led** — bank exposes APIs, brand integrates
- **White-label** — bank product rebadged

## Cash mgmt link

- Treasury behind every embedded finance offering
- Reconciliation across brand platform + bank ledger
- Fund flows: end customer → brand-held account → bank → recipient
- Reg: brand is agent / introducer, not regulated entity (usually)
