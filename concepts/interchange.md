# Interchange

Fee paid by acquirer to issuer per card transaction. Set by scheme.

## Mechanics

- Merchant pays acquirer ~1.5-3% of transaction (Merchant Discount Rate, MDR)
- MDR = interchange + scheme fee + acquirer markup
- Interchange (typically 60-80% of MDR) flows acquirer → issuer
- Funds issuer's economics, drives commercial card rebates

## Regulatory caps (EU + UK)

- **EU Interchange Fee Regulation (IFR) 2015**:
  - Consumer credit: 0.30% capped
  - Consumer debit: 0.20% capped
  - Commercial cards: not capped
- UK: similar post-Brexit
- US: no federal cap (Durbin amendment caps debit only)

## Why commercial cards exempt

- Smaller market, less consumer protection rationale
- Higher interchange = sustainable rebate to corp customer
- Also why virtual cards / P-cards economically attractive

## Cash mgmt revenue impact

- Bank issuer earns interchange revenue stream
- Commercial card desk part of cash mgmt P&L
- Cross-sell with deposit + lending
