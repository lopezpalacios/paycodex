# MMF — Money Market Fund

Short-term debt mutual fund used as cash equivalent investment.

## Types

- **Government MMF** — only government securities
- **Prime MMF** — government + corporate paper
- **Tax-exempt MMF** — municipal (US)
- **CNAV** — Constant NAV (€1.00/unit) — limited post-2017 reforms
- **VNAV** — Variable NAV — most institutional EU funds
- **LVNAV** — Low Volatility NAV — hybrid

## Use in cash mgmt

- Sweep target — overnight deployment of corporate excess cash
- Higher yield than DDA, lower volatility than bonds
- Same-day liquidity (subject to fund-side gates)

## EU MMF Regulation (2017)

- Stricter rules post-2008 / European debt crisis
- CNAV limited to government MMFs
- VNAV / LVNAV for prime
- Daily/weekly liquidity minimums

## Risks

- Credit risk (Lehman 2008 — one prime fund "broke the buck")
- Gate / fee risk during market stress
- Sponsor support — historically common, less reliable now
