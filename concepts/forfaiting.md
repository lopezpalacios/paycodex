# Forfaiting

Sale of medium / long-term trade receivables (often LC- or BG-backed) to a forfaiter at discount, without recourse.

## Mechanics

- Exporter has receivable from importer (often promissory note or accepted draft, LC-backed)
- Sells to forfaiter at discount
- Forfaiter takes credit + country risk
- Exporter gets cash now, full risk transfer

## Vs factoring

- Factoring: short-term, often domestic
- Forfaiting: medium-long term (180 days to 7+ years), cross-border, instrument-based
- Forfaiting always non-recourse

## Use case

- Capital goods exports (heavy machinery, infrastructure)
- Emerging markets where buyer credit hard to assess
- Long-term contracts requiring cash now
