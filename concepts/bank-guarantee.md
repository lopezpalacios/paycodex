# Bank Guarantee (BG)

Bank's undertaking to pay beneficiary if applicant defaults on a contractual obligation. Independent of underlying contract.

## Vs Letter of Credit

- LC = primary payment mechanism
- BG = secondary, paid on default
- Different rules: [[urdg-758]] for BG; [[ucp-600]] for LC

## Common types

- **Bid bond** — guarantees bidder will accept contract if awarded
- **Performance bond** — guarantees contract fulfillment
- **Advance payment guarantee** — guarantees return of advance if non-delivery
- **Warranty bond** — guarantees product quality post-delivery
- **Tax / customs guarantee** — to authorities

## Mechanics

- Applicant requests BG, bank issues to beneficiary
- Beneficiary calls on default (per BG terms)
- Bank pays beneficiary, claims back from applicant
- Demand vs accessory: demand BG = independent (URDG 758), accessory = tied to underlying (rare in international)

## Governing rules

- [[urdg-758]] — Uniform Rules for Demand Guarantees
- ICC Uniform Rules for Contract Guarantees
- Local law (varies)

## SWIFT messages

- MT760 — issuance
- MT767 — amendment
- MT768 — acknowledgment

## Cash mgmt link

- BG facility = credit line
- Provisional collateral on issuance
- Cash impact at call (often years later)
- Off-balance-sheet contingent liability
