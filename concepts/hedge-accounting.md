# Hedge accounting (IFRS 9 / IAS 39 legacy)

Accounting treatment matching gain/loss on hedge instrument with hedged item, smoothing P&L volatility.

## IFRS 9 hedge model (current)

Three types:

- **Cash flow hedge** — hedge variability in cash flows (e.g., forward to cover future receivable)
- **Fair value hedge** — hedge variability in fair value (e.g., interest rate swap on fixed-rate bond)
- **Net investment hedge** — hedge FX risk in foreign subsidiary

## Effectiveness testing

- Qualitative + quantitative
- IFRS 9 simplified vs IAS 39 — allows more economic hedges to qualify
- Ratio: hedge instrument vs hedged item changes
- Effective portion → OCI; ineffective → P&L

## Documentation

- At inception: hedge ratio, instrument, item, risk being hedged
- Ongoing: testing, rebalancing
- Hedge accounting documentation = audit-critical

## Cash mgmt impact

- Treasury hedge program designed with accounting in mind
- Forward dates aligned to forecast cash flow dates
- Documentation effort = ops cost
- Failure to qualify = P&L volatility
