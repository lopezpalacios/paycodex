# Supply Chain Finance (SCF) / Reverse Factoring

Buyer-led financing — supplier gets early payment funded by financier, repaid by buyer at original term.

## Mechanics

- Buyer approves supplier invoice
- Supplier opts in: receive early payment from financier (minus discount)
- Buyer pays financier on original due date
- Buyer's strong credit drives cheap discount rate
- Supplier benefits: cheap working capital
- Buyer benefits: extended terms with supplier (DPO leverage)

## Vs factoring

| Aspect | Factoring | SCF |
|---|---|---|
| Initiated by | Creditor (supplier) | Buyer |
| Credit risk | Factor on debtor (or recourse) | Financier on buyer (anchor) |
| Pricing | Based on debtor credit | Based on buyer credit |
| Disclosure | Varies | Typically open |

## Risk: classification

- 2020s scrutiny on whether SCF should reclassify buyer's payable as debt
- Greensill collapse 2021 highlighted risk
- IFRS / US GAAP disclosure requirements tightened

## Tech / integration

- Buyer ERP feeds approved invoices to platform
- Platform offers to suppliers
- Bank or specialty financier funds
- Settlement back to bank on buyer due date
