# Positive Pay

Check fraud control. Corp tells bank what checks it issued; bank only pays matches.

## Standard positive pay

1. Corp issues checks, sends file to bank (check #, amount, payee, issue date)
2. Checks present at bank for payment
3. Bank matches — exact match = pay automatically
4. Mismatch = exception, corp reviews same day, decides pay or return

## Payee positive pay

Adds payee name match (OCR or scan). Catches altered payee fraud.

## Reverse positive pay

- Bank presents all checks to corp daily
- Corp opts in to pay each one
- Higher operational burden, used when issued-file logistics fail

## Why still relevant

US still writes ~10B checks/year. Check fraud = top fraud type by occurrence per AFP survey.
