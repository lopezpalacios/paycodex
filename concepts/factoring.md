# Factoring

AR financing — corp sells unpaid invoices to factor at discount, gets cash now.

## Variants

- **Recourse** — factor retains right to claw back if debtor doesn't pay
- **Non-recourse** — factor takes credit risk (more expensive)
- **Disclosed** — debtor knows about factor, pays factor directly
- **Confidential** — debtor unaware, pays creditor who forwards to factor

## Mechanics

1. Creditor invoices customer (D)
2. Sells invoice to factor at e.g. 90% face value (D)
3. Customer pays full amount on due date (D+30)
4. Factor takes face value, returns ~5-8% to creditor
5. Net: creditor pays ~2-10% factoring cost for early cash

## Use cases

- SME working capital
- Smoothing seasonal cash flow
- B2B with long payment terms (invoice 60-90 days)

## Tech / integration

- Factor needs invoice data + payment confirmation
- Often integrated with creditor's [[ar-reconciliation]] flow
- Receivables financing platforms: HighRadius, Demica, Greensill (defunct), Taulia
