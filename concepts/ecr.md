# ECR — Earnings Credit Rate

Soft-dollar credit on operating balances, offsets bank fees.

## Mechanics

- Bank publishes ECR (e.g., 3.50%)
- Average collected balance × ECR × (1 - reserve requirement) × (days/360) = earnings credit
- Earnings credit only offsets fees on [[account-analysis]]
- Excess credit does NOT roll forward as cash (some banks allow carry)

## Hard interest vs ECR

- Hard interest = paid as cash, taxable income
- ECR = soft dollar, fee offset only, not cash income
- High-rate environments shift corps toward hard interest accounts (HYSA, MMDA)

## Why banks prefer ECR

- Keeps deposit relationship sticky (LCR-friendly)
- Pays out only if corp generates fees — natural cap
- Cheaper funding than time deposits
